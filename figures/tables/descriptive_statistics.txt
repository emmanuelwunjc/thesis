NOTES
================================================================================

Data Source: HINTS 7 Public Dataset (2022)
- Full Sample: 7,278 adults aged 18 and older
- Analysis Sample: 2,421 observations with complete data for all variables
- Sample Selection: See sample selection flowchart for detailed exclusion criteria

Privacy Caution Index:
- Scale: 0-1 (0 = least cautious, 1 = most cautious)
- Reliability: Cronbach's α = 0.78
- Construction: Mean of 6 sub-dimensions, each based on multiple variables
- Total variables included: 23 privacy-related variables

Regression Models:
- Method: Weighted Least Squares with survey weights
- Dependent Variable: Binary willingness to share data (0/1)
- All models control for demographic and socioeconomic factors
- Standard errors are robust

Significance Levels:
- *** p<0.001 (highly significant)
- ** p<0.01 (very significant)
- * p<0.05 (significant)
- † p<0.1 (marginally significant)

Key Findings:
1. Privacy caution is the strongest predictor of data sharing reluctance (β = -2.32, p<0.001)
2. Diabetes shows a small positive effect but is not statistically significant in main model (β = 0.028, p=0.161)
3. Diabetes moderates the privacy-sharing relationship (interaction β = 0.49, p=0.038)
4. Age has a significant positive effect on data sharing willingness (β = 0.002, p<0.001)
5. Education level shows minimal impact (β = -0.015, p=0.129)
